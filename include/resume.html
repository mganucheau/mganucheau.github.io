Advanced Interaction Design ARTSTUDI264 - Winter 2018 & Winter 2019 :
This upper level studio course will continue and create a sustained investigation into designed interactivity in real space. Students will create interactive installations, or public interventions using sensors or other computational devices.

Drawing with Code ARTSTUDI163 - Fall 2017 :
This studio course will engage coding practices as drawing tools. What makes a good algorithmic composition? How do we craft rule-sets and parameters to shape an interesting work? What changes if we conceive of still outputs, ongoing processes, or interactive processes as the “finished” work? We will look at the history of algorithmic drawing, including analog precedents like Sol LeWitt and other conceptual artists, along with current pioneers like John Simon Jr., Casey Reas, and LIA. Outputs will involve prints as well as screen-based works.

Mechanisms for Urban Engagement DESGN-663-04 - Fall 2018
This is an experimental design studio and the city will be the lab. Students will be introduced, through practical explorations, experimentations, and investigations, to the social, economic and political landscape of San Francisco. Through rigorous research and made work students will question what mechanisms could design create to manifest change? Projects will be created as both solutions and catalysts for change. The class will inquire about human interaction and social discourse and will cover subjects such as urban and participatory design and public and guerrilla art through lectures and reading materials.
